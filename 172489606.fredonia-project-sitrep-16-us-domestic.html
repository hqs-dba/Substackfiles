From our Ambassador: A Day in the Life of the United States

I am sharing excerpts from my personal journal to add to the record about the extraordinarily troubling events I’m observing in Washington D.C.

Fredonia had such a trying 20th century. First the violence of the first World War, then the evil of the Nazis, followed by the brutality of the communists. It was with such hope that we became a democracy. We were so proud to join NATO and be allies with the United States, a country that represented what we wanted to be.

I thought being posted to the United States as the representative of my country would be the highlight of my career. Yet, here we are 8 months into the second Trump administration and it is starting here to feel more like the communism of my youth than the democracy that Fredonia’s government embraced.

Today in Washington, I drove through the city and saw Trump banners hanging from government buildings. It looked like a film set of the Nazi era or a Soviet parade, or perhaps modern-day North Korea.

America was about “for the people, of the people, by the people,” not about one man. America was about separation of powers and checks and balances, yet every day there is a new order from Trump claiming more power. They call it the “Unitary Executive Theory.” Most people on the street call it a monarchy. Did Trump not study George Washington, who turned down the title of King and voluntarily left office when he could have easily won a third term, because the country needed to “move on”? He could have at least watched “Hamilton;” there is a song about it.

And the Trump cabinet members are downright scary: The Secretary of Defense posted classified information on Signal. The Secretary of Homeland Security shows up to paint a fence on the border black (ironically it was the US side of the border, so I am unsure what good that did). The Secretary of Health and Human Services cut millions of dollars of medical research and is an anti-vaxxer. He cut funding for the ongoing MNRA research that saved the world from the worst outcomes of the COVID pandemic . The Secretary of Education can’t pronounce AI. America has some of the greatest minds in the world, yet they are missing from senior government positions. At least Elon is gone. DOGE may have done more harm in a few months than any non-military force to weaken the United States. .

Then there are the immigration raids. There are weapon-toting federal authorities wearing masks who are pulling people off the street. Some of those arrested have legal status, and are being targeted because they spoke or wrote about something. So much for free speech.

It kind of reminds me of the old cold war joke: An American goes up to a Russian and says, “I can stand in front of the White House and say Reagan is an idiot and nothing will happen to me.” The Russian then responds, “I can stand in front of the Kremlin and shout Reagan is an idiot and get paid 5 Rubles.” Except it’s not funny. DC has been taken over by Federal law enforcement and the National Guard. It is very reminiscent of a security state.
