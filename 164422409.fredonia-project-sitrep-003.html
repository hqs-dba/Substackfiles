SUBJECT: SITREP: U.S. POLICY DEVELOPMENTS (17–24 MAY 2025)

SUMMARY:

PRESIDENT TRUMP’S ACTIONS DURING THE REPORTING PERIOD CONTINUED A PATTERN OF UNILATERAL POLICYMAKING THAT STRAINED RELATIONS WITH SMALL AND LARGE DEMOCRATIC ALLIES THROUGH TRADE RESTRICTIONS, DEFENSE FUNDING CUTS, AND CULTURAL PRESSURES.

TRADE POLICY ESCALATION:

— ON 14 MAY, IMPLEMENTATION OF 10% AD VALOREM DUTIES ON CHINESE IMPORTS DISRUPTED SUPPLY CHAINS FOR ALLIES.

— EXPORTERS IN BELGIUM AND FREDONIA FACED COST INCREASES AND LOGISTICAL DELAYS.

— EU THREATENED RETALIATORY TARIFFS WHILE SMALL STATES FACED PRESSURE TO ALIGN WITH U.S. POLICIES.

SECURITY AND DEFENSE PRESSURES:

— EXECUTIVE ORDER ON 20 MAY FROZE 50% OF U.S. CONTRIBUTIONS TO NATO.

— LATVIA PAUSED DEFENSE PROCUREMENT AND SOUGHT ALTERNATIVES FROM FRENCH AND NORDIC SOURCES.

— BALTIC STATES ACCELERATED REGIONAL DEFENSE TALKS OUTSIDE NATO STRUCTURES.

TRANSACTIONAL APPROACH TO ALLIANCES:

— TRUMP'S 23 MAY REMARKS ON UKRAINE IMPLIED CONDITIONALITY BASED ON 'DEALS'.

— PRAISED LEADERS WHO ALIGN WITH U.S. ECONOMIC INTERESTS AND DISMISSED AUTOMATIC DEFENSE COMMITMENTS.

— U.S. RETRENCHMENT FROM DEMOCRACY PROMOTION SIGNALS STRATEGIC UNCERTAINTY.

CULTURAL AND LEGAL COERCION:

— U.S. EMBASSIES THREATENED EU CONTRACTORS OVER DIVERSITY INITIATIVES.

— BELGIAN FIRM ARGENX WARNED ON 18 MAY TO ABANDON GENDER QUOTAS OR LOSE FDA PRIVILEGES.

— PUBLIC BACKLASH AND LEGAL CONFLICTS ESCALATED IN BELGIUM.

DEMOCRACY AID CUTS:

— BY 24 MAY, 83% OF USAID GOVERNANCE PROGRAMS TERMINATED.

— MOLDOVA LOST $15M IN ANTI-CORRUPTION AID, STALLING EU ACCESSION TALKS.

— PRO-RUSSIAN ELEMENTS REGAINED INFLUENCE AMID FUNDING VACUUM.

CONCLUSION:

TRUMP ADMINISTRATION POLICIES DURING THIS PERIOD PRIORITIZED UNILATERAL ACTIONS OVER MULTILATERAL COMMITMENTS, DISRUPTING TRADE, SECURITY, AND DEMOCRATIC DEVELOPMENT IN SMALL AND LARGE ALLIED STATES. REGIONAL ALLIANCES AND REDUCED RELIANCE ON THE U.S. ARE EMERGING AS STRATEGIC RESPONSES.
