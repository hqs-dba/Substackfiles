IV. Legal Context of Treason and Historical Precedent

  • Narrow Constitutional Definition: The U.S. Constitution defines treason very narrowly as "levying War against them, or in adhering to their Enemies, giving them Aid and Comfort." This definition was intentionally made stringent by the Founders to prevent its abuse by political rivals. (Acting Ambassador Comment: not really working, is it?)

  • High Bar for Proof: Conviction for treason requires the testimony of two witnesses to the same overt act or a confession in open court, a significantly higher bar than for most other crimes.

Legal experts consistently dismiss such claims against former presidents for official acts as baseless, citing the Supreme Court's stance on presidential immunity.
