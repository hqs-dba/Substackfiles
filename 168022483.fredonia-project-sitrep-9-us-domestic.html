The BRICS declaration did not name the US or Israel, but condemned the increased tariffs and the military actions against Iran. Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi called for a strong UN condemnation, warning of wider consequences. Russia, however, issued a separate, more direct condemnation of the U.S. and Israel.

He reiterated Iran's commitment to continuing its uranium enrichment and willingness to engage in talks if the U.S. provided assurances against military force during negotiations. This signaled a tactical de-escalation from Iran while maintaining its nuclear ambitions.   • Assessment of Impact of Trump actions on Small Western Democracies, Allied with the US:

The actions by and statements of the Trump administration from July 1-9, 2025, continued to create a challenging and uncertain environment for small Western democracies allied with the U.S. The overarching theme continued to be a reinforcement of "America First" unilateralism, impacting the security, economic stability, and adherence to democratic values of US allies.

  1. Increased Geopolitical Instability and Security Burden:

  • The ongoing fallout from the Iran strikes and the unpredictable nature of U.S. foreign policy continue to generate instability in crucial regions, particularly the Middle East. Small Western democracies, often geographically close or with significant economic ties to volatile regions, face heightened risks of proxy conflicts, terrorism, and disruptions to global energy supplies and trade routes.

  • The expectation that allies will align with U.S. unilateral actions, even without prior consultation, places a greater burden on their defense and foreign policy resources. As the U.S. signals a more transactional approach to alliances, small democracies are pressured to increase defense spending and develop greater strategic autonomy, which can be a significant financial strain. (Acting Ambassador comment: traditional international alliances do not mimic the transitional business type models that Trump seems to prefer in international alliances. Given Trump’s utter failure in the business world - long before his utter failure in managing and running government - we find it hard to imagine that other small democratic countries will be enthusiastic about entering into any kind of pseudo business relationship with Donald J. Trump.)

  1. Economic Uncertainty and Trade Disruptions:

Trump’s obsessive fascination with tariffs threatens the stability of the global economic order and directly impacts small export focused economies. As the global trade environment becomes more volatile, supply chains are impacted, import costs increase, both of which may reduce marked access for goods and services from smaller countries.

  1. Erosion of Multilateralism and International Norms:

Small Western democracies rely on international institutions, rules based orders and norms to increase their stability and undergird their risk taking. Every time Trump follows his “instincts,” and ignores law and rules, he threatens the security of small democracies. His disregard for international consensus makes building broad coalitions to address shared challenges extraordinarily difficult for small democratic nations. (Acting Ambassador comment: Trump’s ignorance of history, philosophies of governance, cultures and geography does not help.)

  1. Challenges to Liberal Democratic Values:

Trump continues to challenge democratic values the world over. Every time he howls about immigration, travel bans or sanctuary jurisdictions he conflicts with human rights and humanitarian principles supported by many small allies of the U.S. This also creates ideological friction which might encourage similar challenges to liberal European democracies.
