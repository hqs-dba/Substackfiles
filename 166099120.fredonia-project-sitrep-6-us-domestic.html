The week of June 8-15, 2025, saw significant actions and reactions surrounding President Donald Trump, highlighted by a military parade in Washington D.C. and widespread "No Kings" protests.

Key Actions and Policy Decisions:

  • Military Parade in Washington D.C. (June 14, 2025): President Trump's long-desired military parade, commemorating the U.S. Army's 250th anniversary and coinciding with his 79th birthday, took place on the National Mall. The parade featured thousands of soldiers, military vehicles including tanks and aircraft, and historical reenactments. The estimated cost of the parade ranged from $25 million to $45 million.

  • Proclamation Restricting Entry of Foreign Nationals (Issued June 4, effective June 9): President Trump issued a proclamation suspending the entry of foreign nationals from 19 countries, citing national security and public safety concerns. Twelve countries faced a "full suspension" for immigrants and most non-immigrants (Afghanistan, Burma, Chad, Republic of the Congo, Equatorial Guinea, Eritrea, Haiti, Iran, Libya, Somalia, Sudan, and Yemen). Seven others faced a "partial suspension" for immigrants and certain non-immigrant visas (Burundi, Cuba, Laos, Sierra Leone, Togo, Turkmenistan, and Venezuela).

  • Other Executive Orders and Actions (Ongoing): While not specific to this week, ongoing policy discussions and previously issued executive orders continued to shape the domestic and foreign landscape. These include efforts to extend 2017 tax cuts, adjust tariffs on Chinese imports, and address issues related to immigration enforcement and federal funding.

Domestic Effects and Reactions:

  • Military Parade:

    • Support: Attendees at the parade largely expressed pride in the nation's strength and military. For many, it was seen as a day to be a "proud American.” The number of attendees was lower than the President predicted.

    • Criticism: Opponents viewed the parade as a politicization of the armed forces, a departure from democratic norms, and an expensive ego boost for the President. Democratic Senator Tammy Duckworth, a veteran, publicly criticized the estimated $30 million cost. Concerns were also raised about potential damage to D.C. streets from heavy military vehicles.


  • "No Kings" Demonstrations (June 14, 2025):

    • Purpose: Coordinated by a coalition of over 200 organizations including MoveOn, the ACLU, and labor unions, the "No Kings" protests were held across hundreds of cities nationwide, explicitly counter-programming the Washington D.C. parade. The movement's theme, "No Kings," aimed to reject what organizers described as authoritarian actions by the Trump administration, "billionaire-first politics," and the militarization of democracy.

    • Scale and Scope: Estimates suggested millions participated in these protests across the 50 states, with large gatherings in cities like Philadelphia (100,000), New York City (50,000), Los Angeles (30,000), and Chicago (20,000). While some local confrontations occurred, organizers emphasized a commitment to non-violent action.

  • Public Sentiment Shifts (Reflected by Demonstrations): The sheer scale and widespread nature of the "No Kings" protests clearly indicated a significant segment of the population felt the Trump administration was overreaching its authority and exhibiting characteristics akin to a monarchy. (Ambassador Comment: The demonstrations reflected a deep-seated opposition to perceived authoritarianism and a strong desire to uphold democratic principles. The comparison of Trump's actions to those of a "king" suggests a public sentiment of concern over the erosion of democratic checks and balances and a fear of unchecked executive power. While not a scientific poll, the visible mobilization signals an increasingly substantial portion of the populace is actively engaged in resisting the administration's direction and is concerned about the future of American democracy.)

  • Immigration Policies: Ongoing federal immigration enforcement raids and the deployment of National Guard troops to cities like Los Angeles in response to protests over these raids drew strong condemnation from Democratic governors, who called it an "alarming abuse of power."

Foreign Effects and Reactions:

  • Travel Restrictions: The June 4 proclamation restricting entry for foreign nationals, effective June 9, would have significant immediate effects on travel and immigration from the listed countries. The stated rationale of protecting U.S. national security and public safety from "foreign terrorists" and those exploiting immigration laws would likely be met with strong reactions from the affected nations and international human rights organizations, potentially straining diplomatic relations and sparking accusations of discrimination. (Ambassador Comment: It seems likely that the Newsom/Trump division over ICE actions in LA may be overshadowing national response to these restrictions. Embassy Fredonia will monitor and report.)

  • Trade and Tariffs: While not directly tied to actions this specific week, the ongoing discussions and potential implementation of Trump's proposed tariffs on goods from Canada, Mexico, and China related to fentanyl, as well as broader national security tariffs on autos, auto parts, steel, and aluminum, continued to be a significant foreign policy and economic concern. The ruling in May 2025 by the U.S. Court of International Trade that some of these tariffs (IEEPA tariffs) were illegal adds a layer of legal complexity and potential for international trade disputes. The overall effect of these tariffs is projected to reduce U.S. GDP and raise costs for American households, and they would likely invite foreign retaliation.

  • Broader Foreign Policy: General statements regarding Ukraine and other foreign policy matters, as seen in past speeches (e.g., questioning continued U.S. funding for Ukraine), will continue to be scrutinized by international allies and adversaries, impacting global stability and alliances.

CONCLUSION:

This period was characterized by a visible polarization within the United States, with a grand display of military power coinciding with significant public protests asserting democratic principles. Internationally, the focus remained on immigration policies and the potential for increased trade protectionism.
