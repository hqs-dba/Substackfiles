On 15 August, 2025, US President Donald J Trump met with Russian President Vladimir Putin at Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson, Anchorage Alaska. The meeting lasted almost three hours, and included issues around the Ukraine War, which started on 24 February 2022, when Russian troops invaded Ukraine. That invasion was unprovoked and illegal. And, has not, yet, resulted in Putin’s desired end: annexation of all or part of the sovereign country of Ukraine by Russia.

No definite deal came out immediately following the summit. Both Trump and Putin noted in their “press conference” that there was “progress,” and “many points were agreed to.” Each also commented that obstacles remained and “the most significant” questions were not fully answered. However, both leaders committed to further pursuit of a negotiated peace to end the Ukraine war, as opposed to pursuing the ceasefire upon which Trump had earlier insisted. While the talks included discussions of territorial concessions and lasting security guarantees for Ukraine, fundamental disagreements remained.
