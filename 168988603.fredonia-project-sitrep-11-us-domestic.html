The Trump Administration: A Week of Trade Escalation and Deepening Crises (July 13-20, 2025)

The week of July 13th to July 20th, 2025, marked a significant escalation in the Trump administration's "America First" agenda, particularly in global trade, creating new crises and intensifying existing ones. The imposition of sweeping new tariffs, coupled with continued strong rhetoric on immigration and a focus on domestic energy, sent ripples of uncertainty across the globe, with particularly acute implications for small, Western, liberal democracies.

Major Actions, Events, and Statements:

  • Tariff Escalation: EU and Mexico Hit Hard (Announced July 12, Effective August 1): The official announcement by President Trump, via Truth Social, of a 30% tariff on all goods imported from both the European Union and Mexico, set to take effect on August 1st. This was framed as a necessary measure to address what the administration termed "long-term, large, and persistent, Trade Deficits" with the EU, and, in Mexico's case, a failure to adequately control fentanyl trafficking into the U.S.

    • Reaction: On July 14, EU trade ministers condemned the 30% tariff as "absolutely unacceptable.” European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen expressed willingness to negotiate but stressed that such tariffs would "disrupt essential transatlantic supply chains." Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum displayed confidence that an agreement would be reached and affirmed national sovereignty.

    • Fentanyl Narrative: The linking of Mexican tariffs to fentanyl flows, despite data suggesting most fentanyl is smuggled by U.S. citizens at ports of entry, underscored the administration's use of immigration and drug trafficking as justifications for its broader policies.

  • Domestic Regulatory Overhaul and Energy Agenda: The administration pressed forward with its agenda of deregulation and promoting domestic energy.

    • "Schedule G" in Federal Service (July 17): An Executive Order established "Schedule G" in the excepted service, allowing the administration to reclassify and potentially dismiss federal employees deemed to be working against the administration's agenda. The Administration is selling this as part of a broader effort to reduce the federal bureaucracy and reshape the civil service. The week's actions, particularly the sweeping tariffs, are poised to have significant and largely negative impacts on the U.S. economy and society.

  • Economic Contraction and Inflation: The 30% tariffs on the EU and Mexico will lead to a substantial increase in US consumer prices across a vast range of goods. This will exacerbate inflation, reduce real wages, and cut into household buying power. Economic models project an average income loss of $2,800 per household in 2025. U.S. real GDP growth is projected to be lower and unemployment is expected to rise.

  • Supply Chain Chaos and Business Disruption: U.S. industries that are closely integrated with European and Mexican supply chains will face immense disruption, higher input costs, and potential loss of competitiveness. The automotive and aerospace sectors will be severely affected.

For small, Western, liberal, democratic nations allied with the U.S., the period of July 13-20 was deeply unsettling, representing a profound challenge to their economic well-being, security, and fundamental values.

  1. Devastating Economic Blow:

  • Direct Tariff Impact: The 30% tariff on all EU imports is an existential threat to many small European economies that are heavily reliant on exports to the U.S. Industries across the economy will face significant losses, leading to widespread job cuts, factory closures, and may experience national recessions.

  • Supply Chain Catastrophe: The tariffs will unravel decades of supply chain integration, leading to massive inefficiencies, increased costs for their own industries, and a sharp decline in trade volumes.

  • Investment Flight: The extreme unpredictability of U.S. trade policy makes long-term investment in these countries highly risky, leading to a flight of capital and further economic contraction.

  1. Profound Erosion of Trust and Alliance Foundations:
