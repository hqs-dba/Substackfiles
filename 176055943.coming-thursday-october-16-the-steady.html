Coming Thursday: The Steady State Assessment of U.S. Democracy

This Thursday at 1:00 PM, The Steady State will release Accelerating Authoritarian Dynamics: Assessment of Democratic Decline — a first-of-its-kind analytic report written by former intelligence and national security professionals.

For decades, our members, as intelligence analysts applied rigorous U.S. intelligence community tradecraft to assess threats abroad. No longer in Government, now we’ve turned that same discipline inward — to evaluate the condition of democracy here at home.

Our findings are sobering.

Drawing on open-source evidence, the assessment identifies clear indicators of accelerating democratic backsliding — consolidation of executive power, weakening of institutional checks, politicization of justice and intelligence, and deliberate erosion of civil-service neutrality. The trend lines point toward what analysts call competitive authoritarianism — a system where elections and courts still exist, but are increasingly manipulated to entrench power.

This document was written by members of The Steady State — former officers of the CIA, NSA, DIA, State Department, and other national-security agencies — using the same structured analytic standards (e.g., confidence levels, key judgments, and scope notes) that guide official intelligence assessments. It is not a partisan argument; it is an analytic warning.

We wrote it because the same tools we once used to assess foreign risks now show unmistakable warning signs at home.
